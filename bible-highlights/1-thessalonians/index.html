Thessalonica, a city on the northern coast of the Mediterranean Sea, was founded in 315 B.C. and named for a sister of Alexander the Great. One of the principal landmarks is the White Tower on the waterfront, therefore a white tower with a lady on top to represent Alexander the Great’s sister, Thessalonica, drawn on the north side of the sea, are the highlights for 1 & 2 Thessalonians.

Since we think of a tower as strong, sturdy, established and safe, you can think that the words “establish, stablish and safety” are used in 1 Thessalonians.

Since Paul instructed the Thessalonians in 1 & 2 Thessalonians on the second coming of Jesus, when you think of the tall tower, you can think of Jesus coming down by them, up in the sky.

1 Thessalonians is the 8th of the 9 books written by Paul to a group of people in a place, Thessalonica, with the book so named, so land is drawn.

(the left side line of the tower can be made into the line going down of a T for Thessalonians and the top line of the tower can be made into the line going across of a T for Thessalonians; the “h” in Thessalonians can be made with the right side line of the tower; so that you can think of these 2 tall letters Th for the tall tower of Thessalonians)

(to remember there are 5 chapters in 1 Thessalonians the top line of the tower can be made into the top line of a 5 for 5 chapters, the left line of the tower can be made into the left line of a 5 and a half circle drawn around the door can be the bottom half circle of a 5 for 5 chapter in 1 Thessalonians)
