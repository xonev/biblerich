(like above) Since Paul was caught up into paradise, a paradise scene of a tropical island with palm trees and the ocean is used for the highlight of 1 & 2 Corinthians.

(like above) The city of Corinth is on a peninsula, almost an island, in the Mediterranean Sea. (like above) Palm trees can be associated with islands and seas, so these are also used to represent 1 & 2 Corinthians.

(like above) Corinth is actually located at about the bottom of the right palm tree. You can think of the right palm tree as pointing to Corinth.

Since this is the second letter written to the Corinthian, you can think of the 2nd palm tree on the left pointing to the spread of the gospel hopefully to the other areas of the peninsula, so the letter would go to them also, which would also give them more light, Biblically speaking, so you can think of the sun in 2 Corinthians.

A sun is drawn in 2 Corinthians because we think of it as being sunny in paradise and because there are 4 familiar verses in 2 Corinthians which mention light, shine, and/or glory.

2 Corinthians is the 3rd of the 9 books written by Paul to a group of people in a place, Corinth, with the book so named, so land is drawn.

(like above - the curved of the land line on the left can be made into a C for Corinthians)

(to remember there is 13 chapters in 2 Corinthians the tree on the left can be made into a 1 for the 1 in 13 chapters and a 3 can be made arching over the tops of the palms tree for the 3 in 13 chapters in 2 Corinthians)
