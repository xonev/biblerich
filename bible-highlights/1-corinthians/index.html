“ Unto the church of God which is at

Corinth”

1:2, “Eye hath not seen, nor ear heard, neither have entered into the heart of man,

the things which God hat prepared

for them that love him.” 2:9

Since the things that God has prepared for them that love him is paradise, a paradise scene of a tropical island with palm trees and the ocean is used for the highlight of 1 & 2 Corinthians.

The city Corinth is on a peninsula, almost an island, in the Mediterranean Sea. Palm trees can be associated with islands and seas, so these are also used to represent 1 & 2 Corinthians.

Corinth is actually located at about the bottom of the palm tree. You can think of the palm tree as pointing to Corinth.

1 Corinthians is the 2nd of the 9 books written by Paul to a group of people in a place, Corinth, with the book so named, so land is drawn.

(the curve of the land line on the left can be made into a C for Corinthians)

(to remember there is 16 chapters in 1 Corinthians the tree can be made into a 1 for the 1 in 16 chapters and a 6 can be made using the line going around the peninsula for the 6 in 16 chapters in 1 Corinthians)
