The first part of 1 Samuel is about Samuel and the last part of 1 Samuel is about Saul being king, so you can think the names - Samuel and Saul - both begin with an S and end with an “l” and 1 Samuel begins with a S and ends with an “l.” You can even think of the number 1 in 1 Samuel as an “l” telling you it is about Samuel and Saul.

The scepter to represent a king in the shape of a 1 is used to help you remember the “l” in Saul and Samuel, and that there are 31 chapters in 1st Samuel, and it is slate blue for Saul who is the king in this book being from the tribe of Benjamin.

The Samuel, Saul, septre, and slate blue begin with an S for Samuel.

(the septre can be made into a long S for Samuel, Saul, septre, and slate blue with the top curve around the ball at the top and making a curve at the bottom. The septre can be made into an “l” for the “l” in Samuel, Saul and slate blue)
