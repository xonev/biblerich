Romans - Roman Column On Land

{% include picture-page-header.html %} {% include media.html %}

“To all that be in Rome” 1:7, “I lay in Sion a stumblingstone and a rock of offence” 9:33, “Let their table be made a snare and a trap, and a stumblingblock” 11:9, “but judge this rather, that no man put a stumblingblock or an occasion to fall in his brother’s way. . . It is good neither to eat flesh, nor to drink wine, nor any thing whereby they brother stumbleth” 14:13, 21

A familiar Roman building structure is a Roman column so it is used for the highlight of Romans.

Since the Roman column is made out of some kind of stone or rock and can also be thought of as a block and in a block shape that you could stumble or stub your toe on, you can remember that Romans is the book that uses the term stumblingstone/stumblingblock/stumbleth the most in the New Testament.

Romans is the 1st of the 9 books written by Paul to a group of people in a place, this one Rome, with the book so named. Therefore, each of these 9 book pictures are drawn with land under them.

Romans was the longest of Paul’s letters so it was placed at the first of his writings.

(the left side of the Roman column can be made into the left side of an R for Romans, the top of the column into the top of the circle of an R, and the right bottom half as the right line going down of an R for Romans)

(to remember there are 16 chapters in Romans the left side of the Roman column can make a 1 for 16 chapters and the right top circle of the Roman column can make the bottom of a 6 for 16 chapters in Romans)
