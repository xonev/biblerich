Philemon - Man Going In House

{% include picture-page-header.html %} {% include media.html %}

“Paul the aged . . . have sent again (Onesimus) . . . receive him . . . Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved” 1:9, 10, 12, 16

Since Paul asked Philemon to receive Onesimus back as more than servant, as a brother, a man going in a house is the highlight for Philemon.

The name Philemon says “fill a man,” so you can think also that if Philemon lets Onesimus back in “a man” will “fill” the house or that it will make Onesimus happy and “fill a man’s” heart.

(the man’s body can be the left line of a P for Philemon and the top line of his head and his curved right arm can be made into the circle top part of the P for Philemon and the next letter in Philemon, “h” can be made by drawing a line on the left side of the house for the left side of a lower case “h” and the top and right side line of the door can make the right side of the “h” in Philemon)

(to remember there is 1 chapter in Philemon, 1 man is drawn going in the house)
