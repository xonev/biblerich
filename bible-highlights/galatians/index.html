Galatians - 2 Swords, 2 Shields & Land

{% include picture-page-header.html %} {% include media.html %}

“Unto the churches of Galatia . . . If any man preach any other gospel unto you than that ye have received, let him be accursed . . . how that beyond measure I persecuted the church of God and wasted it” 1:2, 8, 13, “when Peter was come to Antioch, I withstood him to the face, because he was to be blamed . . . I am crucified with Christ, nevertheless I live” 2:11, 20, “O foolish Galatians, who hath bewitched you . . . Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree” 3:1, 13, “ye would have plucked out your own eyes, and have given them to me” 4:15, “the works of the flesh are . . . witchcraft, hatred, variance, emulations, wrath, strife” 5:19&20

The encyclopedia says the Galatians were always warlike and since Galatians sounds like gladiators (fighters), 2 sword with 2 shields are used for the highlight of Galatians.

Paul wrote the Galatians in response to opponents trying to convince them they had to be circumcised to be saved.

Also the 2 shields look like a bewitching pair of eyes to help remember the familiar verses about bewitched, witchcraft, & plucked out eyes.

Galatians is the book that people deduct Paul’s “thorn in the flesh” from 2 Corinthians 12 was poor eye sight, from the plucked out eyes they wanted to give Paul in Galatians chapter 4 and “how large a letter I have written unto you” in Galatians chapter 6, so the 2 shields as eyes is helpful.

Galatians is the 4th of the 9 books written by Paul to a group of people in a place, Galatia, with the book so named, so land is drawn under the swords.

(the left side of the sword and shield can be made into a G for Galatians, the circles can be made into the “a’s” in Galatians, the top of the two swords can be made into the “l” and the “t” in Galatians)

(to remember there are 6 chapters in Galatians the left side of the sword and shield can be made into a 6 for 6 chapters in Galatians)
